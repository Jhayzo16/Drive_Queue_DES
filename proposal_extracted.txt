C.  O bjectives  of  the  Simulation  
The  simulation  aims  to  achieve  the  following  objectives:  
1.  Analyze  customer  waiting  times  in  the  drive-thru  queue  and  service  windows.  
2.  Identify  bottlenecks  in  ordering,  payment,  and  pickup  processes.  
3.  Evaluate  resource  utilization,  such  as  staff  and  service  windows.  
4.  Test  alternative  scenarios,  such  as  adding  another  order  lane  or  increasing  staff  
during
 
peak
 
hours.
 
5.  Reduce  average  service  and  queue  times  while  improving  system  throughput.  
6.  Measure  overall  system  performance  under  varying  customer  arrival  rates.  
 
D.  Identified  Entities,  Events,  and  Resources  
Entities  
These  are  the  objects  moving  through  the  system:  
●  Customers/Vehicles  
●  Customer  Orders  
●  Queue  (waiting  vehicles)  
Events  
These  are  occurrences  that  change  the  state  of  the  system:  
●  Vehicle  arrival  at  drive-thru

●  Customer  joins  queue  
●  Order  placement  begins  
●  Order  placement  completed  
●  Payment  transaction  begins  and  ends  
●  Food  pickup  begins  and  ends  
●  Vehicle  departs  system  
●  Queue  buildup  or  congestion  event  
Resources  
These  are  components  used  to  provide  service:  
●  Order-taking  station/speaker  
●  Cashier/payment  window  
●  Pickup  window  
●  Service  crew/staff  
●  Drive-thru  lane(s)  
●  Kitchen  preparation  station  
 
E.  Initial  Assumptions  
Since  actual  operational  data  may  not  yet  be  available,  the  following  assumptions  are  
initially

1.  Customer  vehicle  arrivals  follow  a  Poisson  distribution,  averaging  10–15  vehicles  
per
 
hour
 
during

and

2.  Service  times  follow  random  distributions:

○  Order  placement:  1–3  minutes  
○  Payment:  30–60  seconds  
○  Food  pickup:  1–2  minutes  
3.  Vehicles  are  served  on  a  First-In,  First-Out  (FIFO)  basis.  
4.  The  drive-thru  operates  with  one  order  station,  one  payment  window,  and  one  
pickup

5.  Staff  availability  remains  constant  during  a  simulation  run.  
6.  Customers  do  not  leave  the  queue  once  they  enter  (no  balking  or  reneging  
assumed
 
initially).
 
7.  Vehicle  spacing  and  lane  capacity  are  limited,  with  a  maximum  queue  capacity  
(e.g.,
 
8–10

8.  Food  preparation  delays  are  incorporated  into  service  times  unless  explicitly  
modeled

9.  Simulation  will  model  both  normal  demand  and  peak-hour  conditions.  
10.  Random  variability  in  arrivals  and  service  times  will  be  included  to  reflect  
real-world
 
uncertainty.
